Empanadas

Empanadas are a beloved traditional dish in Argentina. These delicious turnovers are made with a flaky pastry dough and filled with a variety of savoury ingredients.

Ingredient & Quantity
2 cups all-purpose flour
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
1/4 cup ice water
1 tablespoon olive oil
1 small onion, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 pound ground beef (or you can use chicken, pork, or a combination)
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1 teaspoon paprika
1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
Salt and pepper to taste
1/2 cup pitted green olives, chopped
2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
Egg wash (1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water) for brushing

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Form it into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until softened and translucent.
  4. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned. Break it up with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.
  5. Stir in the cumin, paprika, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat and let the meat mixture cool slightly. Stir in the chopped olives and hard-boiled eggs.
  7. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  8. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a round cookie cutter or a small plate to cut out circles from the dough.
  9. Place a spoonful of the meat filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over to form a half-moon shape and seal the edges by pressing them together with your fingers or using a fork.
  10. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops with the egg wash.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until golden brown.
  12. Remove from the oven and let the empanadas cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

Note:Empanadas are a popular street food and a staple in Argentine cuisine, enjoyed as a snack or a main course and can be accompanied by a dipping sauce like chimichurri or salsa.
