North American Dishes

North America is the third largest continent. It is bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the east and the Pacific Ocean to the west.

North American cuisine includes a rich and diverse cooking styles. North American cuisines have adapted from European countries, such as Spain, Great Britain and France, along with West African cuisines. North American cooking expanded with the influx of immigrants from nations across the world, and this influx has created a rich diversity of foods and cooking styles.

North America is made up of the United States, Canada, Mexico, Greenland, Jamaica, Cuba, Panama, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, Puerto Rico, Haiti, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, The Bahamas, Nicaragua, Belize, Barbados, Turks and Caicos Islands, Grenada, Guadeloupe, Saint Lucia, Martinique, British Virgin Islands, Antigua and Barbuda, Dominica, Saint Pierre and Miquelon, US Virgin Islands, Anguilla, Montserrat, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Cayman Islands, Saint Bathelemy, Saint Martin, Saint Kitts and Nevis and Saint Marten.

North American has many popular cuisines, including Caribbean dishes, Mexican dishes, American and Canadian classics.
