Feijoada

Considered the national dish of Brazil, feijoada is a hearty black bean stew that reflects the country's rich culinary heritage. This flavourful dish combines black beans with an assortment of pork cuts, such as sausage, bacon, and pork shoulder. Simmered with onions, garlic, and spices, feijoada is served with rice, farofa (toasted cassava flour), collard greens, and zesty orange slices.

Ingredient & Quantity
1 lb (450g) black beans, dried
1 lb (450g) pork shoulder, diced
1/2 lb (225g) smoked sausage, sliced
1/2 lb (225g) bacon, chopped
2 onions, chopped
4 cloves of garlic, minced
2 bay leaves
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
Salt and pepper to taste

Farofa (toasted cassava flour), orange slices, and cooked white rice for serving

  1. Rinse the black beans and soak them in water overnight. Drain and set aside. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the bacon and cook until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the diced pork shoulder and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the chopped onions and minced garlic to the pot and saut√© until the onions are translucent.
  4. Add the soaked black beans, smoked sausage, bay leaves, and enough water to cover the ingredients.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours or until the beans are tender and the flavors have melded together. Stir occasionally and add more water if necessary.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the bay leaves.
  7. Serve the feijoada hot with cooked white rice, farofa, orange slices, and the crispy bacon on top. Enjoy!
