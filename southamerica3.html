Ceviche

Originating from Peru, ceviche is a seafood lover's dream. This vibrant dish features fresh fish or seafood marinated in citrus juices, such as lime or lemon, which lends a refreshing and tangy flavour. Combined with the crunch of onions, the heat of chili peppers, and the freshness of cilantro, ceviche is a perfect balance of flavours.

Ingredient & Quantity
1 lb (450g) fresh white fish fillets (such as sea bass or flounder), cut into bite-sized pieces/div>
1 cup fresh lime juice (about 8-10 limes)
1 red onion, thinly sliced
1-2 rocoto peppers or other spicy chili peppers, seeded and finely chopped
1 tablespoon olive oil
1-2 garlic cloves, minced
1 bunch of cilantro, chopped
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
1 sweet potato, boiled and sliced (optional)
1 ear of corn, boiled and cut into rounds (optional)
Lettuce leaves, for serving

  1. Place the fish pieces in a non-reactive bowl (glass or ceramic). Pour the lime juice over the fish, making sure all the pieces are submerged. Allow the fish to marinate in the lime juice for about 15-20 minutes. The lime juice will "cook" the fish and give it a tangy flavor.
  2. While the fish is marinating, prepare the other ingredients. In a separate bowl, combine the sliced red onion, chopped rocoto peppers, minced garlic, and chopped cilantro.
  3. After the fish has marinated, drain off the lime juice. Add the onion, pepper, garlic, and cilantro mixture to the fish. Gently toss everything together to combine.
  4. Season the ceviche with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.
  5. Let the ceviche sit in the refrigerator for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

    6. Optional:Serve the ceviche with boiled sweet potato slices, corn rounds, and lettuce leaves. The sweet potato and corn provide a nice contrast to the tangy ceviche. Serve the Peruvian ceviche chilled as an appetizer or main dish. Enjoy the refreshing flavors and vibrant taste of this classic Peruvian dish!

Note: It's important to use fresh, high-quality fish when making ceviche. Ensure that the fish is properly cleaned and free of bones before cutting it into pieces. Also, feel free to adjust the spiciness by adding more or less chili peppers according to your taste preference.
